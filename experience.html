Software Engineering Intern

Since October 2013, I have been employed at Bally Technologies Inc. as a Software Engineering Intern. Currently, I am working on completing a game for the popular "Quick Hits" series of slot machine games. This project has allowed me to learn how to adapt to a new development environment. By working with other software engineers, I have been able to learn where certain types of problems would arise within the structure of the development environment. My previous responsibilities have been to assist the game development and operating system teams through the testing of many slot games. I have tested the reliability of the operating systems by modifying configurations of bets and checking that they are in line with the expected outcome. I have also worked to make sure that the games can be used on multiple jurisdictions. Using an FTP server, I was able to test for the New York jurisdiction whether or not certain games could be downloaded over the network onto new hardware. I have improved my communication ability by working with members of the development teams to solve many problems that have arisen.

Mobile Engineering Education Lab (ME2L)

Between November 2012 and October 2013, I was employed with ME2L as a student instructor. The purpose of the ME2L program is to educate young students, typically in elementary or middle school, about engineering in an attempt to inspire interest in joining an engineering field in the future. A typical lesson would be comprised of teaching a 30-40 minute lesson on an engineering topic followed by a 15-20 minute exercise where the students get to experience engineering principles. After teaching the lesson, my role would be to help the children connect what we just taught them to the exercise they are working on. This job taught me excellent teamwork and communication skills, as well as improved my ability to explain complex concepts in a simple way so that my audience could understand the topic.
